Building at the Intersection

When I started my journey as a pilot, I never imagined I'd also become a software engineer and entrepreneur. But the skills transfer: precision in the cockpit translates to precision in code. Checklist discipline becomes sprint planning. Weather decision-making becomes product pivoting.

The best builders I know come from unexpected backgrounds. They bring fresh perspectives to old problems. They see connections others miss.

Lessons from 500+ Hours in the Cockpit

Flying teaches you things no classroom can. When you're solo at 12,000 feet over the Rockies, there's no one to ask for help. You prepare, you decide, you execute. Every single time.

These are the lessons that shaped how I approach business: thorough preparation, calm under pressure, respect for complexity, and knowing when to ask for help before it's too late.

A Year in Spain: What I Learned About Business and Culture

Living in Bilbao changed how I see the world. The Spanish approach to business—relationships first, transactions second—felt foreign at first. Now I realize it's the secret to building things that last.

Fluency isn't just about words. It's about understanding context, reading between the lines, and building genuine connections across cultures.
